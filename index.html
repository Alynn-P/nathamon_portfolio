A 5-step workflow from raw data to actionable customer groups — built for a luxury brand where RFM simply doesn't work.
01 / 05
Context
The Brief
Fine Jewelry Brand · Customer Segmentation Assignment
While working at a fine jewelry brand, I was assigned to develop a customer segmentation model to support strategic planning tailored to each customer group.

This project outlines my workflow — from understanding the data to generating actionable segmentation insights that the business could act on immediately.
CHALLENGE
Luxury price variance from thousands to millions THB makes standard RFM models unreliable
SOLUTION
CLV-based segmentation capturing long-term value, purchasing intent and lifecycle behavior
02 / 05
Methodology
The 5-Step
Workflow
01
Data Foundation
Understand the Data
Review all available data dimensions to assess their potential value for segmentation.
  • Bill amount
  • Product details
  • Order date
  • Customer information
  • Channel
02
Data Profiling
Explore the Metrics
Extract descriptive statistics from selected variables to build data intuition.
  • Average spending per bill
  • Average lead time between bills
  • First purchase gap
These insights serve as a foundation for choosing suitable models and segmentation axes — ensuring any segmentation is grounded in real-world patterns.
03
Model Selection
Choose the Model — CLV
CLV = Margin × Retention Rate ÷ (1 + Discount Rate − Retention Rate)
RFM was excluded due to the luxury variance in product price ranging from thousands to millions THB. In a fine jewelry business, purchase frequency does not necessarily reflect customer value. Instead, CLV-based segmentation was applied to capture long-term customer value, purchasing intent and lifecycle behavior more accurately.

03 / 05
Finding 01
5 Customer Groups
Identified
Mapped on CMV vs. Avg. Growth on Bill Axes
Group 1 · Jewelry Lovers
High-value loyal customers who consistently purchase high-ticket items. This group demonstrates strong brand affinity and long-term value.
Best engaged through relationship-focused strategies such as premium loyalty programs or early-access privileges.
Group 2 · Potential Upscale Group
Customers with more than 4 purchases and a mix of high- and low-value transactions in their first 4–5 bills.
The negative average growth suggests room to shift them into the top-tier group.
Ideal for promotional offers, luxury sampling or strategic upselling.
Group 3 · Emerging Segments (most densely populated)
This group splits into two key subgroups based on behavioral patterns.
3.1 Customers with 4+ purchases who alternate between high and low-value bills
3.2 Customers with frequent purchases of lower-value items
Although their CLV has not yet crossed the bill threshold, short purchase frequency suggests strong brand engagement.
May benefit from graduated incentives.
Group 4 · Occasional High Spenders
Customers with 4+ bills, starting with a high-value purchase followed by a significant drop.
The negative average growth actually reveals solid purchasing power.
Best targeted for reactivation or event-driven marketing campaigns.
Group 5 · One-Time Buyers
Customers with only one transaction. Data profiling shows those who don't return within 2 years are unlikely to return at all.
Best treated as cold leads; however, selective reactivation could be tested for those with a high initial spend.

Context
The System Brief
Closed-Loop Agricultural Decision System
This project explores a closed-loop agricultural decision system designed to optimise fertiliser timing using environmental data. By integrating weather forecasting, soil moisture inference, and feedback from real farm outcomes, the system continuously learns and improves its recommendations — aiming to reduce fertiliser loss and increase crop productivity.
CORE PROBLEM
Fertiliser applied at the wrong time — before rain or drought — leads to significant waste and yield loss
APPROACH
Rule-based decision engine driven by weather + soil data, with an ML feedback loop that learns from actual yield outcomes
KEY INNOVATION
The loop — farm outcomes feed back into ML training, making the system smarter with every harvest cycle
SYSTEM TYPE
Closed-loop · Rule-based + ML hybrid · Real-time environmental data integration
